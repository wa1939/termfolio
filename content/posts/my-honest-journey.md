## Hello to all my readers!

I'm here to tell you a story about change, habits, and some hard truths. You know those books, right? "The Power of Habit," "Atomic Habits," and others like them? I used to read them thinking, "These are good ideas, but not for me." I'm always up for learning new things, but changing my whole routine? That seemed too much.

![Habits cover](/images/blog/habits-cover.png)

## The Day It Hit Me

My life was like a loop. Wake up, work, home, sleep. Then one day, I thought, "Is this it? Am I doing things right?" I knew something had to give.

## Starting With a Simple Step

It all began with the Fajr prayer. I set this unique alarm that only turned off after I solved a puzzle. This meant no more hitting snooze and lying in bed. It was the first step in my journey to change.

### Waking Up Early: Not So Fun

Trust me, getting out of bed early wasn't cool at first. And sleeping early? Super hard for a night person like me. But I kept thinking about what I read in "The Rich Employee": You've got to work hard for success.

### My Office Hours Got Crazy

Coming to work early gave me some quiet time before others arrived. However, it also meant my workdays became longer. Colleagues would hand me tasks late in the day, thinking I had plenty of time left, just like them. But hey, that's part of trying to get better, right?

### It's Not All Fun

Honestly, not every part of changing habits is fun. There were days I just wanted to chill and do nothing. But I kept reminding myself of my goals. I wanted to grow, to succeed. So, I kept at it, even when it was tough.

### Finding Joy in the New Routine

Despite the challenges, I found ways to enjoy the new routine. The post-gym cold pool was a game changer -- trust me. Swapping my music for podcasts while driving? Turned out to be pretty neat.

### Summing Up My Experience

So, here's what I've learned from my journey. Transitioning from a guy who doubted the power of routines to someone who's found a harmonious balance in life wasn't easy, but it's been incredibly rewarding. The key is to start with small changes and stay committed. You can make these changes too, one step at a time. Let's take these steps together and turn our lives into something really cool, one habit at a time!

Catch you in the next one!
