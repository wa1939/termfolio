Hey everyone! Lately, I've been swamped -- work, daily life, and yep, I've got a PMP exam on March 10th. You know how it goes, right? When you need to study, suddenly everything else seems super interesting. So, I had this idea: why keep my blog on super.so, when I could try making my own website? I stumbled upon this thing called [Next.js Notion Starter Kit on GitHub](https://github.com/transitive-bullshit/nextjs-notion-starter-kit#nextjs-notion-starter-kit).

![PMP distraction](/images/blog/pmp-distraction.jpeg)

Now, let me be honest -- I didn't know the first thing about Next.js, TypeScript, or React. But, that just made me want to learn it even more!

For the past two weeks, it's been a wild ride of learning and experimenting. I've been glued to **ChatGPT** and **Copilot**, trying to figure out how to tweak the code. And guess what? I actually got my Notion page turned into a website! But if I'm being real with you, it's not exactly what I wanted yet. I think I can make it better. For now, though, I've got to hit pause and focus on my PMP exam. But once that's out of the way, you bet I'm going to spruce up that website.

![Progress](/images/blog/pmp-progress.png)

And here's a little heads-up for you all -- in the coming weeks, all my posts are going to be about project management. As I learn, I'll share my insights with you, right up until the big reveal when I (hopefully) tell you that I've passed the PMP! So, stay tuned for some project management wisdom, and let's embark on this journey together.
